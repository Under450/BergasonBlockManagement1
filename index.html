Who We Are

Bergason Property Services Limited is a premier independent property management firm dedicated to the seamless administration of residential blocks across the UK.

With decades of collective experience, we bridge the gap between complex property law and practical, everyday estate management. We pride ourselves on transparency, integrity, and a proactive approach to building maintenance and leaseholder relations.

Key Compliance Areas

  • Service Charges: Transparent budgeting and certification.
  • Section 20: Statutory consultation management.
  • Buildings Insurance: Reinstatement valuations and claims.
  • Fire Safety: Fire Safety Act 2021 compliance.
  • RTM Support: Right to Manage process guidance.

Our Services

Complete day-to-day management including site inspections, ground rent collection, cleaner/gardener supervision, and 24/7 emergency support.
Full formation and statutory notice process for Right to Manage companies.
Accurate service charge budgeting, arrears recovery and certified year-end accounts under RICS and ICAEW regulation.
Vetting, tendering, supervision and insurance verification for all contractors.
Managing the full statutory consultation process for qualifying works exceeding £250.
Comprehensive safety compliance including FRAs, EICRs, asbestos monitoring and H&S.
Management of large-scale refurbishment projects to ensure cost and quality control.
Online portal for issue reporting, updates, downloads and document access.

Garside v RFYC (2011)

  • Reasonableness includes financial impact.
  • Phasing may be required for affordability.
  • Urgent repairs override cost concerns.
×

Waaler v LB Hounslow (2017)

  • Repairs vs improvements distinction.
  • Financial hardship must be considered.
  • Consultation must be meaningful.
×

Holland v Oxford CC (2002)

  • Leaseholders entitled to original invoices.
  • Reinforced inspection rights under LTA 1985.
×

Francis v Phillips (2014)

  • Cannot split works to avoid consultation.
  • Total cost must be considered.
×

Arnold v Britton (2015)

  • Clear lease wording prevails.
  • Fairness does not override contract terms.
