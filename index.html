Garside v RFYC (2011)

This case confirmed that service charges must be “reasonably incurred” and that leaseholder affordability is a relevant factor in determining fairness.

  • Landlords must consider financial impact on leaseholders.
  • Phasing works may be required.
  • Urgent works may proceed even if expensive.
  • The Tribunal can assess proportionality and necessity.
×

Waaler v LB Hounslow (2017)

A leading case distinguishing between repairs and improvements.

  • Repairs are obligatory; improvements discretionary.
  • Leaseholder financial circumstances must be considered.
  • Consultation must be meaningful, not procedural.
×

Holland v Oxford CC (2002)

Strengthened leaseholder rights under Section 22 LTA 1985.

  • Landlords must allow inspection of supporting invoices.
  • Prevents vague or hidden accounting.
×

Francis v Phillips (2014)

Clarified qualifying works requirements and Section 20 thresholds.

  • Projects must be viewed as a whole, not split into parts.
  • Artificial division to avoid consultation is unlawful.
×

Arnold v Britton (2015)

Leading Supreme Court case on strict contractual interpretation.

  • Clear wording must be upheld, even if unfair.
  • Courts will not rewrite leases.
