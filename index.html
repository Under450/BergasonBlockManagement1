Garside v RFYC (2011)

This Upper Tribunal decision confirms that service charges must be “reasonably incurred” under Section 19 of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985. Key principles include:

  • Leaseholder affordability is a relevant factor in determining reasonableness.
  • Landlords may be required to phase major works to reduce immediate burden.
  • Urgent works may proceed even if costly.
  • The Tribunal may consider proportionality, necessity, and financial impact.
×

Waaler v LB Hounslow (2017)

This Court of Appeal case established:

  • A distinction between repairs (obligatory) and improvements (discretionary).
  • Leaseholders’ financial circumstances must be considered in improvements.
  • Landlords must consult meaningfully and not treat consultation as a formality.
×

Holland v Oxford CC (2002)

Expanded rights of leaseholders to inspect documents supporting service charge demands.

  • Landlords must allow inspection of invoices, receipts and vouchers.
  • Strengthens Section 22 Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 obligations.
  • Prevents landlords from hiding expenditure behind summaries.
×

Francis v Phillips (2014)

A key case relating to qualifying works and Section 20 consultation.

  • Works must be viewed as a whole, not subdivided artificially.
  • Landlords cannot “split” projects to avoid consultation requirements.
  • Clarifies the threshold and meaning of qualifying works.
×

Arnold v Britton (2015)

A landmark Supreme Court decision on contract interpretation.

  • Clear lease wording must be upheld, even if burdensome.
  • Courts cannot rewrite contracts to protect leaseholders from harsh terms.
  • Reinforces importance of strict contractual interpretation.
