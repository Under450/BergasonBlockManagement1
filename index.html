Why Choose Us?

Bergason Block Management offers a complete professional property management service for freeholders, residential management companies and property developers.

With over 37 years in the property and legal industry, we focus on customer service, ensuring dedicated attention to your property needs.

We operate a 24hr, 7 day a week service for emergencies. This ensures all issues are dealt with and hopefully abated before further issues arise.

Our clients often experience a 15-25 percent reduction in expenditure within the first year which directly leads to lower service charges.

As a family-run business, we prioritise face-to-face communication and swift responses to address any concerns our clients may have.

We offer tailored solutions allowing you, the freeholder or directors, to make discretionary expenditure decisions without the heavy administrative burden often found elsewhere.

Whether you require full hands-on management or a more controlled service, Bergason adapts to your needs. A director personally oversees your block or steps back as required.

Changing block management companies is not the logistical nightmare many believe. Our transition process typically takes 5 to 7 days.

The services that might typically be covered, but not limited to the Block Management services we provide are:

  • Preparing and distributing service charge budgets/estimates.
  • Collecting service charges.
  • Preparation of invoices prior to examination by independent accountants.
  • Providing information to auditors for annual accounts.
  • Collecting routine service charge arrears.
  • Liaising with residents and associations.
  • Employing contractors and arranging quotes.
  • Inspecting the property and handling non-major repairs.
  • Periodic health and safety checks.
  • Holding annual resident meetings if required.
  • Record-keeping of residents and tenancy details.
  • Corporate governance support for RMC/RTM companies.

Fees typically range between £14 to £20 + VAT per unit per month.

We always recommend a free site inspection to assess the development and prepare a tailored management plan.

A breakdown of additional fees outside the core package (e.g. Section 20 works) can be provided once instructed.

If you would like any further information, call us or arrange for a director to meet on-site to discuss requirements.

Garside v RFYC (2011)

This case examined whether costs were “reasonably incurred” under s.19 Landlord and Tenant Act 1985.

  • Leaseholder affordability is relevant
  • Works may require phasing
  • Urgent repairs may proceed even if expensive
  • Tribunals may adjust unreasonable charges
×

Waaler v LB Hounslow (2017)

Established key distinctions between repairs (mandatory) and improvements (discretionary).

Leaseholder finances must be considered during consultation.

×

Holland v Oxford CC (2002)

Confirmed leaseholder rights to inspect invoices and supporting documents under Section 22 LTA 1985.

×

Francis v Phillips (2014)

Splitting works artificially to avoid Section 20 consultation is unlawful.

Arnold v Britton (2015)

Confirmed strict interpretation of contractual lease terms even if the results are harsh.
