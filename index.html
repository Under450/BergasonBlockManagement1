Garside v RFYC (2011)

Key findings from the Upper Tribunal:

  • Service charges must be “reasonably incurred” under LTA 1985 s19.
  • Leaseholder affordability CAN be a factor.
  • Works may need to be phased to avoid unreasonable burden.
  • Urgent works may proceed regardless of cost.
×

Waaler v LB Hounslow (2017)

  • Distinction between repairs (mandatory) and improvements (discretionary).
  • Leaseholder financial views must be considered.
  • Consultation must be meaningful.
×

Holland v Oxford CC (2002)

  • Leaseholders have the right to inspect underlying invoices, receipts and vouchers.
  • Strengthened rights under LTA 1985 s22.
×

Francis v Phillips (2014)

  • Qualifying works must be examined as a whole, not split artificially.
  • Landlords cannot divide contracts to avoid S20 consultation.
×

Arnold v Britton (2015)

  • Clear contractual wording must be enforced, even if harsh.
  • Courts will not correct a “bad bargain”.
