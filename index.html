Garside v RFYC (2011)

This case examined “reasonably incurred” costs under s.19 Landlord and Tenant Act 1985. The Tribunal held that affordability is relevant and works may require phasing.

  • Leaseholder affordability is relevant
  • Works may require phasing
  • Urgent works may proceed even if expensive
  • Tribunal may adjust unreasonable costs
×

Waaler v LB Hounslow (2017)

Established distinction between repairs (mandatory) and improvements (discretionary). Consultation must be meaningful and consider leaseholder finances.

×

Holland v Oxford CC (2002)

Reinforced leaseholder rights under Section 22 LTA to inspect invoices and supporting documents.

Francis v Phillips (2014)

Confirmed that splitting works artificially to avoid S20 consultation is unlawful.

Arnold v Britton (2015)
