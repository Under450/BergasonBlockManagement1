Garside v RFYC (2011)

This appeal considered whether service charge costs were “reasonably incurred” under section 19 of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1985. The Tribunal confirmed that:

  • Affordability for leaseholders is relevant
  • Phasing of works may be required
  • Urgent works may proceed regardless of cost
  • Tribunals may adjust costs that are disproportionate
×

Waaler v London Borough of Hounslow (2017)

This landmark Court of Appeal judgment distinguished between repairs and improvements. Landlords must:

  • Consult meaningfully with leaseholders
  • Consider leaseholder financial circumstances
  • Apply different tests to improvements versus repairs
×

Holland v Oxford City Council (2002)

A key case reinforcing transparency. Leaseholders have the right under section 22 LTA 1985 to inspect invoices, receipts and vouchers supporting service charges.

Landlords must provide genuine facilities for inspection.

×

Francis v Phillips (2014)

The Court of Appeal held that works should be viewed as a whole when assessing whether they exceed the £250 per leaseholder threshold requiring Section 20 consultation. Landlords:

  • Cannot artificially split works to avoid consultation
  • Must consider total cost of the project
×

Arnold v Britton (2015)

A leading Supreme Court judgment confirming strict interpretation of lease clauses, even where outcomes appear harsh. Courts will not rewrite a “bad bargain”.
