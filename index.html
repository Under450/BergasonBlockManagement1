Garside v RFYC (2011)

Key principles from the Upper Tribunal:

  • Service charges must be “reasonably incurred” under Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 s19.
  • The tribunal must consider the *impact on leaseholders* including affordability.
  • Phasing works over several years may be required.
  • Urgent works may proceed despite cost concerns.
×

Waaler v LB Hounslow (2017)

  • Distinction between repairs (mandatory) vs improvements (discretionary).
  • Leaseholder financial circumstances must be considered for improvements.
  • Consultation must be meaningful, not procedural.
×

Holland v Oxford CC (2002)

  • Leaseholders entitled to see underlying invoices, receipts and vouchers.
  • Strengthened rights under Section 22 Landlord and Tenant Act 1985.
×

Francis v Phillips (2014)

  • Works must be considered as a whole for Section 20 consultation.
  • Landlords cannot split contracts to avoid consultation requirements.
×

Arnold v Britton (2015)

  • Clear contract wording prevails even if harsh.
  • Courts will not “fix” a bad bargain.
